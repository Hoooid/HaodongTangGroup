          <article class="np-research-card">

            <div class="np-research-card-copy">
              <span>01</span>
              <h3>Colloidal Quantum Dot SWIR Detection</h3>
              <dl>
                <dt>Core Question</dt>
                <dd>How can solution-processed PbS quantum dots deliver low dark current, strong carrier extraction, and stable short-wave infrared response?</dd>
                <dt>Our Approach</dt>
                <dd>Ligand exchange, interfacial layers, hybrid-size transport strategies, and process routes that tune band alignment and suppress leakage paths.</dd>
                <dt>Representative Outcome</dt>
                <dd>Recent work advances inverted PbS QD photodetectors, interface-enhanced smoke detection, and SWIR devices with improved transport layers.</dd>
                <dt>Related Papers</dt>
                <dd class="np-paper-links">
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1109/led.2026.3658433">LiF/C60 inverted PbS QD PD</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jpclett.5c01032">QD size effects for SWIR HTLs</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1109/led.2025.3532323">PVDF-enhanced SWIR PDs</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1021/acsphotonics.4c01864">Hybrid-size QD HTLs</a>
                </dd>
              </dl>
            </div>
          </article>

            <div class="np-research-card-copy">
              <span>02</span>
              <h3>Perovskites And Low-Dimensional Optoelectronic Materials</h3>
              <dl>
                <dt>Core Question</dt>
                <dd>How do crystal quality, dimensionality, traps, and phase evolution shape the optoelectronic response of emerging semiconductors?</dd>
                <dt>Our Approach</dt>
                <dd>Controlled growth, defect and trap-density reduction, perovskite-to-QD conversion, and material routes that bridge visible and infrared operation.</dd>
                <dt>Representative Outcome</dt>
                <dd>Perovskite single-crystal thin films and converted PbS QDs support cleaner transport pathways for next-generation photodetectors.</dd>
                <dt>Related Papers</dt>
                <dd class="np-paper-links">
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.xcrp.2023.101363">Perovskite single-crystal films</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1002/adom.202402740">Perovskite nanocrystals to PbS QDs</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1002/sdtp.17368">CsPbBr3 phase transition</a>
                </dd>
              </dl>
            </div>
          </article>

          <article class="np-research-card">

            <div class="np-research-card-copy">
              <span>03</span>
              <h3>Photodetector Arrays And Imaging</h3>
              <dl>
                <dt>Core Question</dt>
                <dd>How can single-pixel device performance be translated into array-level uniformity, image quality, and application-ready sensing?</dd>
                <dt>Our Approach</dt>
                <dd>Patterned thin films, array-oriented process control, device-to-system testing, and application demonstrations such as infrared imaging and smoke detection.</dd>
                <dt>Representative Outcome</dt>
                <dd>The group builds a path from high-performance photodetectors toward imaging arrays while drawing on microdisplay and pixel-integration experience.</dd>
                <dt>Related Papers</dt>
                <dd class="np-paper-links">
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1021/acsaelm.5c01168">Instant smoke detection</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1002/jsid.1253">QD-on-silicon microdisplay</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nanoen.2020.105254">Spray-deposited QD solids</a>
                </dd>
              </dl>
            </div>
          </article>

            <div class="np-research-card-copy">
              <span>04</span>
              <h3>CMOS And Chip-Compatible Integration</h3>
              <dl>
                <dt>Core Question</dt>
                <dd>What material and interface choices make colloidal optoelectronic devices compatible with practical chip and readout processes?</dd>
                <dt>Our Approach</dt>
                <dd>Self-assembled monolayers, chip-aware process windows, low-temperature thin films, and interface stacks that support scalable integration.</dd>
                <dt>Representative Outcome</dt>
                <dd>CMOS-compatible PbS QD photodetectors connect material chemistry to integrated-device constraints.</dd>
                <dt>Related Papers</dt>
                <dd class="np-paper-links">
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.26599/NR.2025.94907796">CMOS-compatible PbS QD PDs</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1002/jsid.1253">QD-on-silicon integration</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1109/ogc62429.2024.10738747">Electrophoretic deposition for SWIR</a>
                </dd>
              </dl>
            </div>
          </article>

            <div class="np-research-card-copy">
              <span>05</span>
              <h3>Device Physics, Characterization And System Validation</h3>
              <dl>
                <dt>Core Question</dt>
                <dd>Which measurements actually explain device limits, and how should emerging photodetectors be compared across materials and labs?</dd>
                <dt>Our Approach</dt>
                <dd>Standardized EQE thinking, transient and spectral measurements, in-situ structural probes, interface analysis, and application-level validation.</dd>
                <dt>Representative Outcome</dt>
                <dd>Recent work argues for better testing standards while earlier studies use GISAXS, spectroscopy, and transport analysis to connect structure to performance.</dd>
                <dt>Related Papers</dt>
                <dd class="np-paper-links">
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41928-025-01446-7">EQE testing standardization</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1021/acsami.9b00889">Two-step ligand exchange</a>
                  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1021/acsami.0c12732">QD-gold interface formation</a>
                </dd>
              </dl>
            </div>

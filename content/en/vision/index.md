                <span>01</span>
                <h3>Colloidal Quantum Dot SWIR Detection</h3>
              </div>
              <div class="np-research-detail-grid">
                <section>
                  <h4>Core Question</h4>
                  <p>How can solution-processed PbS quantum dots deliver low dark current, strong carrier extraction, and stable short-wave infrared response?</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Our Approach</h4>
                  <p>Ligand exchange, interfacial layers, hybrid-size transport strategies, and process routes that tune band alignment and suppress leakage paths.</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Representative Outcome</h4>
                  <p>Recent work advances inverted PbS QD photodetectors, interface-enhanced smoke detection, and SWIR devices with improved transport layers.</p>
                </section>
              </div>
            </div>

                <span>02</span>
                <h3>Perovskites And Low-Dimensional Optoelectronic Materials</h3>
              </div>
              <div class="np-research-detail-grid">
                <section>
                  <h4>Core Question</h4>
                  <p>How do crystal quality, dimensionality, traps, and phase evolution shape the optoelectronic response of emerging semiconductors?</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Our Approach</h4>
                  <p>Controlled growth, defect and trap-density reduction, perovskite-to-QD conversion, and material routes that bridge visible and infrared operation.</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Representative Outcome</h4>
                  <p>Perovskite single-crystal thin films and converted PbS QDs support cleaner transport pathways for next-generation photodetectors.</p>
                </section>
              </div>

              </div>
              <div class="np-research-detail-grid">

                  <h4>Our Approach</h4>
                  <p>Patterned thin films, array-oriented process control, device-to-system testing, and application demonstrations such as infrared imaging and smoke detection.</p>

                <section>
                  <h4>Representative Outcome</h4>
                  <p>The group builds a path from high-performance photodetectors toward imaging arrays while drawing on microdisplay and pixel-integration experience.</p>

                <span>04</span>
                <h3>CMOS And Chip-Compatible Integration</h3>
              </div>
              <div class="np-research-detail-grid">
                <section>
                  <h4>Core Question</h4>
                  <p>What material and interface choices make colloidal optoelectronic devices compatible with practical chip and readout processes?</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Our Approach</h4>
                  <p>Self-assembled monolayers, chip-aware process windows, low-temperature thin films, and interface stacks that support scalable integration.</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Representative Outcome</h4>
                  <p>CMOS-compatible PbS QD photodetectors connect material chemistry to integrated-device constraints.</p>
                </section>

                <span>05</span>
                <h3>Device Physics, Characterization And System Validation</h3>
              </div>
              <div class="np-research-detail-grid">
                <section>
                  <h4>Core Question</h4>
                  <p>Which measurements actually explain device limits, and how should emerging photodetectors be compared across materials and labs?</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Our Approach</h4>
                  <p>Standardized EQE thinking, transient and spectral measurements, in-situ structural probes, interface analysis, and application-level validation.</p>
                </section>
                <section>
                  <h4>Representative Outcome</h4>
                  <p>Recent work argues for better testing standards while earlier studies use GISAXS, spectroscopy, and transport analysis to connect structure to performance.</p>
                </section>
              </div>
